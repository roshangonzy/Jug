Q6pH Scale Math
Difficulty: H

Solution A has a pH of 4. Solution B has a pH of 6. Which statement is correct?

Your Answer

Solution A has a lower concentration of $\ce{H+}$ ions.

Correct Answer

Solution A is 100 times more acidic than Solution B.

Explanation: The pH scale is strictly logarithmic. This means that every single step on the scale represents a ten-fold (10x) difference in hydrogen ion concentration. The difference between a pH of 4 and a pH of 6 is exactly two steps. Therefore, you calculate $10^2$, meaning Solution A is exactly 100 times more acidic than Solution B.

Test 1: Action Plan

1

Conquer the 'Salts' Taxonomy & Hydrolysis

You lost marks misidentifying Normal, Acid, and Basic salts. Rule: Acid Salts still have replaceable $H^+$ ions ($\ce{NaHSO4}$). Basic Salts still have replaceable $OH^-$ ions ($\ce{Pb(OH)Cl}$). Normal Salts have completely lost all $H^+$ and $OH^-$ ($\ce{Na2SO4}$). For Hydrolysis, the strongest parent wins (e.g., $\ce{Na2CO3}$ comes from a strong base and weak acid, making it Alkaline).

2

The "Metallic vs. Non-Metallic" Oxide Rule

Rule: Metallic Oxides = Basic (They dissolve to form bases). Non-Metallic Oxides = Acidic (They dissolve to form acids). Furthermore, all alkalis are bases, but NOT all bases are alkalis. An alkali is strictly a water-soluble base like $\ce{NaOH}$.

3

pH Math & Acid Fact-File

Every 1 step on the pH scale is a 10x difference in $H^+$ ions (A pH of 4 is 100 times more acidic than pH 6). Memorize fast facts: Formic acid is in ant stings, Sulphuric acid is in car batteries, and Human Blood is slightly alkaline (~7.4).
